Our Beliefs

ARTICLES OF FAITH

We, the members of ECWA, uphold the following beliefs and practices:




The sixty six books both Old and New Testaments are the inspired word of God, without error in the words of the original writing,the complete revelation of God's will for the salvation of men, and final authority for all Christian faith and practice (2 Tim. 3:16; 2 Peter 1:21; 1 Cor. 2:13).

There is one God, the creator and Preserver of all things, infinite in being and perfection. He exists eternally in three persons; Father, Son and Holy spirit who are co-equal (Deut. 6:4, Matt 28:19; Heb. 1:1-13; Col. 1:15,19).

Jesus Christ is very God and very man, having been conceived of the Holy Spirit and born of the virgin Mary (Luke 2:3-35; Phil. 2:5-8; 1 Cor. 15:1-4; Heb. 1:3).

The Holy Spirit is the Third person of the God-head who regenerates, indwells, baptize scale, and fills those who believe(John 14:16,17; Eph. 1:13-14; 1 Cor. 12:12-13)

God originally created an innumerable company of sinless, Spirit beings, known as Angels (Is. 14:12-17; Ezek. 26:11-19).

Man was created in the image of God and likeness of God but in Adam all mankind fell into sin with the result that all men are sinners by nature and action. (Gen. 1:26;27; 2:27; 26:5).

Salvation is recieved by faith alone in the God-man Jesus Christ, apart from works.(Acts 4:12; Jn. 1:12).

Everyone who truly believes in Jesus Christ atoning work and has appropriated this benefit personally to life is assured of Salvation both now and forever.(Col. 2:2; 1 John 5:13; Heb. 6:11; 10:22; John 5:24; 10:28).

That is believers' setting apart unto God is three fold: positional at conversion; progressive on daily basis and ultimate in heaven. (John 17:17; 2 Cor. 3:18; 7:1; Eph. 4:3-10, 30).

The church embracing all true believers is the Body and Bride of Christ, formed by the baptism of thre Holy Spirit. (Eph. 1:22-23; 1 Cor. 12:13; Phil. 1:1; Eph. 4:3-10; 30).

The Ordinances of the Church are the Lords supper and water baptism to be observed by the Church during the present age. (Matt. 28:19; Luke 22:19-20; Acts 8:36-38; 10:47-48; 16-32).

Christians are called to a holy life of service and testimony in the Holy Spirit, which service including the propagation of the power of the Gospel message to the whole world. (Acts 18:8; 1 Cor. 3:12-15; 2 Cor. 5:9-10).

Those are bestowed free as determined by the Holy Spirit on Believers and not by the will or desire of man for the edification of the Body of Christ and for service to the Glory of God. (Rom. 12:6; 1 Cor. 12:4-11; Eph. 4:11).

The blessed hope of the Church is the personal, Imminent and pre-millennial second coming of the Lord Jesus Christ, which is the rapture of the believers, both the living and the dead. (John 14:1-3; 1 Cor. 15:52; 1 Thess. 4:13-18).

In the end times shall come a period of divine judgement upon the whole world. (Matt. 24:15-21; Jer. 30:7).

The dead will eventually resurrect bodily at the end times, believers into everlasting blessedness and joy with their Lord, unbelievers into judgement and everlasting conscious punishment. (John 11:12-26; Luke. 16:19-26; 23:42; 1 Cor. 15:20-22).

These spiritual gifts are all given by the Person of the Holy Spirit to every true believer in Christ. They are gifts of the Holy Spirit given for the Purpose of edifying (building) the body of Christ not for self edification and for the glory of God only. (Roman 12:3-8; 1 Cor. 12:3-11; Eph. 4:11-16; 1 Peter 4:9-11).
